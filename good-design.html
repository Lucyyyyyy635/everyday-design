Good Design Example: Simple Toggle Switch Effects

This webpage demonstrates good design through simple toggle switch effects, reflecting key ideas from Everyday Design as a Design Resource. Just as everyday design repurposes common objects for new functions, using familiar elements like toggle switches ensures intuitive interactions. The clear visual contrast between “on” and “off” states enhances usability, making actions easy to distinguish at a glance. This approach mirrors the study’s emphasis on adapting everyday artifacts for practical use, highlighting that good design often relies on simplicity, familiarity, and clear communication.

Green Button

Dark Red Button

Black Button with Panda Icon

Grey Button (OFF)

Yellow and Orange Gradient Button (YES)

The warm hues of this button could be used to indicate positive responses in customer satisfaction surveys or feedback forms. It's also appropriate for any scenario that requires a clear affirmative answer.

Reddish-Orange Button (NO)

The vivid color design intuitively conveys refusal or disagreement, making it suitable for voting, opting out of a service, or rejecting terms.

Red Button (No)
